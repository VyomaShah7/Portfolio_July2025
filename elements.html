Abstract

This thesis presents a comprehensive simulation for 6G enabled smart city designs by integrating the smart city concepts of 5G and the 6G technology aspects including latency, scalability, energy efficiency, storage and micro cell concepts. This research proposes a multi layered hierarchical system that consists of smart IoT devices, edge nodes, fog clouds, centralized cloud, base station and server to address the limitations of current 5G based smart city infrastructures[64]. The simulation enables real-time and predictive analytics, secured data handling, efficient disaster discoveries and responses, latency and delay aspects of the working and the warnings and inspections of the IoT devices on regular updates. Four smart city fire disaster management scenarios are applied that includes a scenario with normal working without all connections, one with normal working and all the connections, one with fire disaster response and warning with all connections, and one with fire disaster response and handing escape route response with a compromised point of contact and all the rest of the connections intact to demonstrate the improvements in latency, energy consumption, fault recognition, fault handling and rerouting, fault recovery and bandwidth optimization. The research is focused on benchmarking the potential and highlights of 6G technologies that can improve and build faster responsive and more resilient smart cities along with the maintained security and privacy.
